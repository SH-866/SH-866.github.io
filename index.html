Puzzle Cube

Planning two different puzzle cubes with five pieces each. One is an easier difficulty, and the other is a harder difficulty. The cube is 3x3x3 cubes. Sketches done for both. Using Inventor to 3-D model each cube piece individually and assemble for both difficulty cubes. Forming a physical puzzle cube with wooden blocks glued together with wood glue and collecting data on solve times and critiques. This was only done for the harder cube.

Reflection

Conclusion

Overall, the cube performed well. However, the solve rate of the cube was 50%, with 5 of 10 testers solving it. Additionally, 4 of 5 of those who did solve it did it in over 5 minutes, which was the ideal maximum solve time. In terms of difficulty, this puzzle cube is shown to be too difficult for the requirements. In terms of construction, for the most part the cube fit together well, however there were some pieces that weren't quite flush.

CAD Train

Following instructional videos to assemble a train in Inventor. Modeling each piece, learning how to use a CAD software and how to use the tools provided Assembling the pieces together to learn ow to combine pieces in 3-D printing design.

Results

Conclusions

Overall, the process was straight forward. Some of the videos were more difficult to follow than others, but overall the process was manageable with the help of other students and the course teachers. It was a valuable experience in learning CAD design.
